📓 Hike Journal

Every trail has a story. Here's ours — step by step, peak by peak.

Moderate 📅 March 22, 2025 🧍 4 hikers 📍 8.4 km · 620 m gain

Misty Morning on Mt. Apo Trail

We started before dawn, headlamps flickering through the mist. The fog was so thick you could almost drink it — the air smelled of pine resin and wet moss. About 2 km in, we heard the first bird calls echoing off the ridge. By the time we reached the mid-point saddle, the sky was turning tangerine and the whole valley dropped away below us.

Gia almost slipped on the mud just past the bamboo grove — she held on to a root and laughed it off. That's the thing about this group: we fall, we laugh, we keep going. Ate lunch at the summit rocks: rice, canned tuna, and overripe mangoes. Best meal I've had in months.

Moderate ⏱ 4 h 35 min moving time
Easy 📅 February 8, 2025 🧍 6 hikers 📍 5.2 km · 180 m gain

Waterfalls & Wildflowers Loop

We had planned this one for months, waiting for the right season when the wildflowers bloom along the riverbank. The day delivered. Within the first kilometre we spotted three different species of orchid clinging to the mossy stones.

The third waterfall — a 20-metre curtain of white water — was the highlight. We sat below it on flat rocks and ate trail mix, barely speaking. Sometimes nature just shuts you up in the best way. The whole crew made it, including Kuya Ben who had just recovered from a sprained ankle. He was slower, we were slower together. Nobody minded.

Easy ⏱ 2 h 50 min moving time
Hard 📅 January 1, 2025 🧍 3 hikers 📍 14 km · 1,400 m gain

New Year's Sunrise Summit

We camped at the treeline on New Year's Eve — the stars were outrageous. Sleeping bag temperature: −3 °C. Actual temperature: well below that. We woke at 4 AM and scrambled the last 600 metres of gain in the dark. Ice on the rocks, fingers numb inside gloves.

Then 6:12 AM: first light. The horizon cracked open in orange and pink, the summit bathed in gold. We stood there in total silence — three friends at 2,800 metres, watching 2025 begin. No fireworks needed. Nothing left to say. Just the wind, the light, and the feeling that everything is possible.

Hard ⏱ 9 h 10 min moving time
Moderate 📅 December 7, 2024 🧍 5 hikers 📍 10.8 km · 850 m gain

Cloudforest Traverse

Above 1,600 m the trees get short and gnarled, draped in thick moss that turns every branch into a sculpture. Clouds roll through at eye level — you're literally walking inside a cloud. Visibility drops to 20 metres. You follow your friends by sound.

We found a tiny camp spot in a natural hollow, boiled water from a spring, and made hot ginger tea. Laughter. Ghost stories. Biscuits. A perfect night.
