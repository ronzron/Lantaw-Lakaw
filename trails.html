Ridgeline Loop

Moderate
8.4 km
Distance
620 m
Elevation Gain
4–5 h
Duration
1,240 m
Max Elevation
Loop
Route Type

The Ridgeline Loop is our most-hiked trail. Starting from the pine forest trailhead, it climbs steadily to the main ridge where sweeping views open up on both sides of the mountain range. The return leg follows a different ridgeline so you never retrace your steps.

Best visited October–April. Bring at least 2 L of water — no sources on the ridge. The path is well-marked with orange blazes. Suitable for hikers with moderate fitness.

2–3 h
Duration
680 m
Max Elevation
Out & back
Route Type

One of the most family-friendly hikes in the area. You follow the cool mountain river upstream through a lush canyon, with multiple shallow crossing points and natural pools along the way. Three small waterfalls can be reached as side-trips.

Great year-round but especially beautiful just after the rains when the foliage is intensely green. Sandals or water shoes recommended at river crossings. Excellent first hike for beginners.

Duration

Max Elevation
Out & back
Route Type

The flagship challenge of the Lantaw-Lakaw group. The Summit Scramble gains 1,400 metres over 7 km of rugged trail before a final exposed rock scramble to the summit block. The views from the top are nothing short of spectacular — on a clear day you can see three provinces.

Recommended for experienced hikers only. Register with the ranger station, carry a headlamp for early starts, and never attempt in bad weather. Camping at the saddle (km 6) splits the climb into two days for a more enjoyable experience.

Cloudforest Traverse

Moderate
10.8 km
Distance
850 m
Elevation Gain
6–7 h
Duration
1,780 m
Max Elevation
Point-to-point
Route Type

A magical point-to-point route through a mossy cloudforest. Above 1,600 m the trees become gnarled and draped in thick green moss, clouds sweep through at eye level, and the forest becomes almost surreal. Best done with an overnight at the forest camp mid-route.

Requires two cars or a shuttle arrangement. Trail is marked but can be muddy — trekking poles and gaiters strongly recommended. Carry plenty of warm layers; temperatures drop sharply after dark.
